title: "Drilling Monitoring System"
excerpt: "Condition monitoring of the bottom hole assembly (BHA) is crucial throughout the drilling lifecycle — from planning and real-time execution to post-job analysis. Among its components, the mud motor remains underutilized as a source of insight, even though it significantly influences downhole dynamics and drilling
performance. A key research objective is to develop an intelligent monitoring framework that distinguishes torque contributions from the drill bit and the drillstring. This capability is critical for diagnosing drilling inefficiencies, such as poor hole cleaning or tight spots, and for optimizing performance. Initial results comparing surface-measured and simulated downhole torque indicate strong agreement, while deviations correlate with wellbore condition changes — highlighting the need for automated mode recognition, real-time mud motor torque estimation, and physics-informed modeling. Building on these findings, my research will integrate field data, data analytics, and physics-based models to create an advanced condition monitoring system for BHAs. A central component of this framework will also focus on automated monitoring and control of drilling fluid properties and drilling hydraulics, as they play a fundamental role in cuttings transport and wellbore stability. By incorporating real-time fluid property measurements and hydraulic modelling into the decision-making process, the system will provide a real-time advisory, improving efficiency and reducing the risk of downhole complications. This approach will enhance drilling performance, reduce non-productive time, and accelerate the transition toward more autonomous and intelligent drilling operations.<br/><img src='/images/drilling_data.png'>"
